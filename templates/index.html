Funding Rate
Perpetual futures funding rate on Binance BTC-PERP. Paid every 8 hours between longs and shorts to keep the perp price anchored to spot.
> +0.01% → longs pay shorts — bullish crowding, mean-reversion risk
< −0.01% → shorts pay longs — bearish crowding, squeeze risk
≈ 0 → balanced; funding neutral
💡 Extreme positive funding often precedes short-term reversals. The bot adds a bearish score when funding is very positive.
Raw CVD (Cumulative Volume Delta)
Running total of (buy volume − sell volume) on Binance BTC perp trades since the current 15-minute window opened. Measures who is being aggressive.
Positive (+) → market buys dominate — bullish pressure
Negative (−) → market sells dominate — bearish pressure
Threshold: |CVD| > ~20 = meaningful signal
💡 CVD divergence from price is a key tell: price rising but CVD falling = weak rally likely to reverse.
Deep OFI (Order Flow Imbalance)
Aggregated order flow imbalance across the top 10 price levels of the Binance order book. Captures whether resting liquidity is being added or pulled on each side.
> +5 → strong buy-side order flow
< −5 → strong sell-side order flow
Score threshold: |OFI| > 15 for a full +1/−1 signal
💡 Deep OFI leads price moves by 1–3 seconds. Combined with CVD it forms the strongest short-term directional signal in the engine.
VPIN Proxy
Volume-synchronized Probability of Informed Trading. Estimates what fraction of recent volume came from informed (directional) traders vs noise traders.
> 0.70 → toxic flow detected — blocks weak signals
0.40–0.70 → mixed; use other signals
< 0.40 → low informed flow; market is noisy
💡 High VPIN means someone with information is trading hard. The engine blocks entries when VPIN > 0.70 on weak signals to avoid adverse selection.
OBI (Order Book Imbalance)
Top-of-book imbalance: (bid_size − ask_size) / (bid_size + ask_size) at the best bid/ask on the Binance BTC order book.
+1.0 → all liquidity on bid side — strong buy pressure
−1.0 → all liquidity on ask side — strong sell pressure
Signal fires: |OBI| > 0.3
💡 OBI is the fastest signal in the engine — it updates every cycle from live L2 data. Use it as a directional tie-breaker when other signals are mixed.
Spread Pressure
Measures spread compression on the Polymarket YES side. A narrowing spread combined with strong order book imbalance suggests a directional move is forming.
Compressed → spread < normal; momentum forming
Normal → typical spread width; no signal
Wide → spread > 8%; market quality gate triggers
💡 If the spread is > 8%, the engine skips the entire cycle — you can't get a fair fill. Check this when trades aren't executing.
Oracle Alignment
Compares the Chainlink BTC/USD oracle price on Polygon with the live Binance spot price. A divergence > 0.15% suggests the oracle hasn't updated yet.
Aligned → divergence < 0.15%; oracle is fresh
Lagged → divergence > 0.15%; oracle may be stale
Score boost: oracle lag in your direction = +0.5 edge
💡 When the oracle lags behind a fast BTC move, the Polymarket market hasn't fully repriced yet. This is a short-lived but real edge.
bVol Multiplier (Belief Volatility)
Measures how fast the model's posterior probability is oscillating in the current window. High belief volatility means the model is uncertain and noisy.
1.0–1.15 → calm; posterior is stable
1.15–1.30 → moderate oscillation; slight dampening
> 1.30 → high noise; posterior pulled toward 50%
💡 When bVol is high, even a strong posterior (e.g. 80%) gets pulled toward 50% by the Bayesian update. This prevents overconfidence in choppy windows.
BTC Price Source
Current BTC spot price, computed as Strike + Distance. Distance is the difference between the latest Binance BTC/USDT price and the strike price for this 15-minute window.
Primary: Binance BTCUSDT spot (via 5m klines)
Fallback: Coinbase BTC-USD candles
Updates: Every cycle (~5 seconds)
💡 The price shown is the latest 5-minute candle close from Binance. If Binance is down, Coinbase is used. The "distance" (Δ) is what matters — it determines if BTC closes above or below strike.
Strike Price Source
The BTC price at the exact start of the current 15-minute window. This is the "line in the sand" — if BTC closes above this, YES wins; below, NO wins.
Priority 1: Binance 15m kline open price
Priority 2: Coinbase 15m candle open
Priority 3: Binance 5m kline nearest to window start
Source shown: --
💡 Strike is locked once resolved and never changes within a window. The source label below the price shows where it came from. Binance 15m open is most reliable.
Z-Score
Standard deviations the current BTC price is away from its recent mean, measured over the last 20 candles. Captures how stretched or compressed price is relative to its recent range.
0 to ±1 → price within normal range
±1 to ±2 → mild extension, mean-reversion possible
> ±2 → strong extension; increases posterior conviction
> ±3 → extreme; high-confidence directional bias
💡 A negative Z-Score with a DOWN signal means BTC is extended below its mean — the Bayesian model assigns higher probability to continuing down or snapping back. The Z-Score feeds directly into the posterior calculation.
Expected Move ($)
The one-sigma expected BTC price move for the 15-minute window, derived from the ATR (Average True Range). Represents how much BTC would need to move to be considered a "normal" swing in this window.
Formula: ATR14 × 1.125 (scaled for 15m window)
Low ($50–80) → tight, choppy market
Normal ($80–150) → typical volatility
High (>$200) → trending or news-driven
💡 The expected move sets the scale for what counts as a meaningful directional push. If BTC is already near the expected move boundary, the model de-risks — a reversal is statistically more likely.
σ_B (Bayesian Sigma)
The uncertainty width used in the Bayesian likelihood calculation. Derived from ATR × bVol multiplier — it sets how "wide" the model's prior is when interpreting BTC price distance from strike.
Low (0.10–0.20) → narrow prior, high conviction signals
Normal (0.20–0.35) → balanced uncertainty
High (>0.40) → wide prior, posteriors pulled toward 50%
💡 σ_B is the engine's "humility knob." When bVol is high (oscillating signals), σ_B widens and the posterior shrinks toward 50% — preventing overconfident trades in noisy markets.
Target Edge
The model's estimated profit edge on the best side (UP or DOWN). Computed as: Posterior − Market Price. Represents how mispriced the market is relative to the model's probability estimate.
< 3.5% → below threshold, no trade
3.5%–6% → minimum viable edge, small size
6%–12% → good edge, normal sizing
> 12% → strong edge, monster signal possible
💡 The required edge threshold (req) adjusts based on regime and balance. Edge must exceed this threshold for a trade to fire — it is the primary go/no-go gate after score.
Market Price (YES Mid)
The current mid-price of the YES contract on Polymarket for this 15-minute BTC window. Computed as (best bid + best ask) / 2 from the live Polymarket order book.
0.50 → market sees 50/50 chance
< 0.40 → market leans DOWN strongly
> 0.60 → market leans UP strongly
Wide spread (>8%) → illiquid, engine skips cycle
💡 The model's edge = posterior − market price. If the market already prices in the move, there's no edge to capture. Trades only fire when the model disagrees with the market by at least the required edge threshold.
